DIGITAL FORENSICS

The process of identifying, preserving, analyzing, and presenting digital evidence in a forensically sound manner to support investigations and legal proceedings.

Identification and Preservation

The phase in digital forensic investigation where potential sources of digital evidence are identified and secured in order to prevent alteration, destruction, or loss of the evidence.
Analysis

This phase involves examining the preserved digital evidence to identify and interpret relevant information that can provide insight into the incident and assist in drawing conclusions.

Reporting and Conclusion

This step involves presenting a detailed report of the investigation findings to the stakeholders and drawing conclusions based on the evidence gathered, which can be used for legal action or to improve security measures.

TOOLS OF TRADE



01. Autopsy®

Autopsy® is an open-source digital forensic tool used for analyzing and investigating various types of digital media, including hard drives, memory dumps, and mobile devices. It offers a user-friendly interface and a wide range of features, such as keyword searches, file carving, and email analysis, to help investigators quickly identify and recover relevant data.

02. VOLATILITY FOUNDATION

Volatility is a popular and open-source memory forensics tool used for analyzing the memory dump of a running system, such as a computer or mobile device. It allows investigators to identify and extract valuable information from the system's memory, including running processes, open files, network connections, and user activities.

03. Magnet ACQUIRE

Magnet Acquire is a digital forensic tool used to acquire data from various types of digital media, including hard drives, mobile devices, and cloud storage. It offers a user-friendly interface and supports a variety of acquisition methods, including logical and physical acquisitions, and the ability to capture volatile memory.
